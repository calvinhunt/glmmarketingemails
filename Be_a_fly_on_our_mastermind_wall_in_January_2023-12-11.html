If you've ever thought about joining one or our in-person groups, now is the time, and Brian and I would like to give a few of you a chance to try our January meeting out.

 

 

At one of our recent meetings this is what we learned:

 

  1. None of the HERO members are wholly reliant on any one media. We had a good discussion of some insider news about Google’s next move and what we can do for each other to prepare and immunize our websites. 

  1. Many are making themselves famous in some niche not necessarily directly related to a practice area. This is by design and scheme, not accidental.  

  1. Many have made at least one very high-end video that is a centerpiece but just as many are killing it with much more "casual" videos shot on iPhones, (but this has to be done right.)  

  1. Most have tightly crafted intake scripts and they are recording (and listening to) their calls. This has become MANDATORY for quality control.

  1. Almost all have some personal “challenge,” so don’t feel bad if you do, too. The truth is that there aren't too many place where you could actually share and get good feedback on this subject, in a confidential, non-judgmental "safe space."

  1. Many are using at least one contract employee/virtual assistant, in addition to their full-time staff, in their practices. 

  1. Some love their PPC managers; others still struggle to get that ad media to work profitably. Importantly, we talk about and compare vendors a lot.

  1. All agree, that for reasons none of us can explain, the “recipe” portion of the mailed monthly newsletter is the most read and reacted to section!  

  1. They all know and cultivate their top referral sources. 

  1. The most common issue amongst the group is the problem of “scaling up.” These practices produce leads and cases. The big issue is building a business that can keep up with the marketing. 

  1. Neither practice areas nor geography seem to be limiters to practice growth. This group is diverse and successful no matter what.
