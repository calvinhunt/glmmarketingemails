About 15 firms were in town last week for our quarterly in-person mastermind meeting. The typical firm in the group is doing about $400,000 - $1,000,000, though some are larger. What is amazing amongst attorney mastermind groups is the diversity of practice areas in this group. While Brian and I are injury and disability lawyers, we have HERO Mastermind members who are creating great lives for themselves, their families and their teams in practice areas such as:

 

  1.  "The Business Lawyer for Lawyers"
  2. IP Law;
  3. Personal Injury in states such as Iowa, Virginia, Illinois;
  4. Business litigation;
  5. DUI defense;
  6. Tax Defense;
  7. Family law;
  8. Labor and employment;
  9. Business Law;

There are others and of course, some of the members firms practice in more than one area of law.

 

Varied interests in the law, but commonality as well. All are seeking that practice and lifestyle that allow them to

 

  1. do work they love,
  2. with people they like;
  3. while being compensated well for it and
  4. freeing up time to pursue other interests outside of law:

 

Our purpose when we meet is simple: we work on each other's business.

 

(Next week our ICON group comes in - generally speaking, those are practices with top line revenue, generally, in the $1,000,000 - $5,000,000 range.)

 

Each member gets what we call a "hotseat," and the format for a hotseat is generally:

 

  1. Update on the firm and the commitments you made last time you were here (or intro, if you are a first time attendee);
  2. A "share" of something that you have found to benefit your life. This ranges from ideas like books, podcasts, technology, fitness tips and yes, other "life hacks." (We learned the secret to assembling the Lego Dinosaur, which apparently can be tricky to put together!)
  3. A discussion of what it is you want the group to help you with.

Yesterday, the "I need help" topics included "asks" on:

 

  1. Vendors, especially those in the digital marketing space (if you are a vendor to the legal market and you have helped or hurt a member, know that we are having open discussions about you);
  2. Compensation models for all level of team members;
  3. Out of country workers (and the companies that are matching lawyers with outsourced help)
  4. Pricing models for fees
  5. Referral network building;
  6. "of counsel" relationships
  7. Firm culture;
  8. How to analyze where the money is coming from;
  9. Best way to learn new business skills
  10. email management
  11. annoying clients
  12. how to write more compelling print and e-newsletters
  13. how to get rid of admin work
  14. how to move from 'lawyer" to CEO

Of course, there are many conversations that take place over breakfast, lunch, dinner (all of which is included in the meeting) and on breaks. These members commit to "putting down their devices" and playing "full in" during these meetings. (Note, however, that this group is not about how to try cases, pick juries, talk to judges, brag about verdicts, etc - plenty of other groups for that.)

 

Since Brian and I were just back from our own 2-day offsite law firm retreat, we also went, line by line, over our financial and other firm goals for 2024-2034. Members saw our numbers, broken down by practice area, the average value of each of our case types, how we have built one area of the practice that delivers over $600,000 annually of monthly, recurring revenue from clients who say with us for years, and an inside look at what we each are good at and not good at. We showed our mastermind members what each member of the Ben Glass Law leadership team has committed to for Q1 and encouraged each ot them to model something like this for their firms.

 

And that was just day one, which was capped by a fantastic dinner out, where the discussions continued. We are part way through day two as you are reading this email.
