Today is a "plain and simple truth" kind of day.

It may be a hard truth for some.

But it's the truth. Inviolate and assuredly.

The truth is many practice "owners" work for their law firms.

The law firm is calling the shots for the owner, instead of the other way around.

(How does that make sense?)

It's a problem I help attorneys deal with every day, which is the reason I know it to be true. More attorneys than I can count have opened up to me about this particular frustration...

"Ben, I feel like I'm no longer in control."

Michael Gerber's famous book, E-Myth, explains the idea this way: Do You Work IN Your Business or ON Your Business?

When you work IN your practice, you are an employee. Your daily rhythms come less from your personal motivations than a sense of demands placed on you by the practice.

(Most of these "demands" are imagined, if I'm being honest. It's why I always tell attorneys to simply ask the question, "Who made that rule?")

Successful Attorneys Work ON Their Practices

The best practice-owners I know take the business side of their law firm very seriously.

Every day, they look for a way to improve the practice, whether it through better marketing or getting more productivity from employees or creating a system to manage an unwieldy process.

The put the practice to work FOR THEM, instead of going to work for the practice.

It's a big shift.

And a critical one to make if you are serious about living a better life.

I think being a law firm owner is a great decision, by the way. It's the first big step in taking control of your life - becoming an entrepreneur.

The biggest mistake, however, is letting that control slip away.
