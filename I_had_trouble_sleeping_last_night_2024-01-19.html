Next month begins my 20th year of running Great Legal Marketing. 

 

Twenty year of running mastermind groups, doing events, hosting webinars (or, the "old" days, "teleseminars," and creating intellectual property that, when applied to a life and a law practice, will help a lawyer accept that life is meant to be lived in happiness, not sadness, and show him/her what others have done to archive that life.

 

Leading Lawyers to a Life Filled with Happiness

 

If I've done anything in/for the profession at all, it's been being able to gather together in one room lawyers who believe they have a right to their own happiness and want to know how to get their. Since there are not many places where you can go around talking about "my own happiness," as a primary goal, we have built something very special. One by one, we are improving the profession, and doing it without so-called "Wellness" CLE's.

 

Yesterday our highest level in-person mastermind group got together in our training center in Fairfax, VA,  for our Q1 meeting. About 15 firms were represented, many having endured some pretty significant travel challenges to get here.

 

A Lot has Changed

 

Twenty year of running mastermind groups. Technology and topics have changed a lot since we began. Back in the day ALL of the discussions were about marketing, and the biggest questions were "how do we make a better Yellow Page ad and "what's this Internet thing?"

 

Ranking high in the search engines was really easy because so few lawyers had eve begun to have websites and yes, keyword stuffing worked really well for a bit.

 

YouTube has Been Around a Long Time but...

 

Yesterday's discussions were different. Yes, some marketing. Ian Garlic not only brought us up to date on the power of YouTube, but he showed us what looks to be "right around the corner" in terms of the huge opportunity for lawyers. 

 

Some of the members shared their "hacks" for using AI to get good, quality, authentic, content out the door with the help of A.I. 

 

Revenue Breakthroughs

 

We talked about each others numbers, and several members broke through revenue thresholds of $2, $3 and $4 million. 

 

We spent a bunch of time talking about how each member finds team members who can get behind the vision and mission of the firm. 

 

What happens in a well-formed mastermind is that when an idea is shared, often the next 5-10 ideas from THAT idea just begin popping up from around the table as "the mastermind effect" takes over the room. (Kind of hard to describe if you have never been in room like this.) 

 

By the end of the day, each person has long list of ideas to sort through, even those lawyers like Michele Lewane  and Beth and Corey Walker, who have been in the group for 10+ years.
