Ask Different Questions if You Want Better Answers

 

On one our local listserves, a lawyer asked one of those “how much do you all pay your paralegals, I want to know that I am in the right ballpark” questions.


Here was my private answer back to him (slightly abbreviated here for space):


WARNING: Deep thinking ahead… not your simple fill-in-the-blank answer!!!


OK, you know I’m an outlier with all this.


Non-traditional, spitting into the wind, fully believe that a law firm is built to serve my interests first, allow me to do exactly what I want to do, when I want to do it.


The “traditional bar” doesn’t like what I have to say about law and business, but who cares, I’ve got enough people who do like to hear what I have to say and they pay to hear my advice on this and other matters.

 

Our clients are very well served and we make good money…


I don’t pay at the highest range of the scale.

 

I have the best teams in both of my businesses I’ve ever had (BGL and GLM) and everyone who works for me will tell you (1) it’s the best place they’ve ever worked; and (2) they have no plans to leave.


How do we get there?


1. Being very clear in my own head WHY I’m here (on earth) and WHY I’m running a law business (My purpose) – to inspire others to live life big (clients, employees, vendors…everyone).


2. Being very clear on how we interact with the world (My Values) – no bullshit, no games, no threats.


3. NEVER engaging in typical lawyer nonsense…Your reputation (our reputation) is golden and I want to keep it that way.


4. Being very clear what our Mission is, what goals are we trying to hit and by when?


5. Communicate 1, 2 and 3 to a pool of good people out there and say, “We know we aren’t for everyone, but there are LOTS of good people out there and most, if working, are working in toxic environments


6. This creates a smaller pool of people who WANT to come work here.


7. Be very patient with hiring. Hire Slow and Fire Fast, never out of panic.


8. Continually train to your core values, monitor and track everything


9. Record all calls, listen to samples of them


In other words, money becomes a secondary issue to a person’s life purpose
So, for me, the wrong question is, “how much do they get paid?”

 

The better question is: have you built something that people would actually want to join before they know the salary?

 

Ben
