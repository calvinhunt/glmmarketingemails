Lawyers think about cost all the time. 

 

Nothing wrong with it.  But price alone is meaningless without value.

 

Here's what I'm talking about: lately we've been calling firms who have registered for the GLM Summit.  We want to make sure they have an amazing experience so we're confirming that they've squared away their hotel rooms, suggesting that they come in a day early (Florida weather can be crazy), and that they hang out a day late (to decompress from all of the ideas and to decide on their next action item).

 

And here's the scary thing we're learning.

 

Something like a quarter to a third of the firms who have registered for this event are still using a phone tree for a receptionist...

 

These are firms interested in business building, coming to us to learning the latest in digital marketing, and intending to build a high-profit, low-maintenance law firm... but they are hemorrhaging money at the front desk.

 

We hear this all the time - that firms try to increase profit by keeping costs low.  

 

But let's do some math... how many clients do you have to lose to the phone tree before you could have paid the receptionist?  

 

In my firm: three.  

 

As long as having a live person answering the phone gets me three more clients than the phone tree does, the receptionist pays for herself.

 

Duh, right?

 

Well, it isn't obvious to everyone.
