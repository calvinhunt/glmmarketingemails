Planning and executing on an event like the Great Legal Marketing Summit takes over a year (we have already been working on Summit ‘24) and it is exhausting. We “worry” about things like: 

  1. Will we still be interesting to those who have been showing up, year after year, for over a decade? 

  1. Will someone who just found us a week ago find us interesting enough to stay around for a long time? 

  1. Will the vendor/sponsor partners feel like they got their money’s worth for the investment of time and money to have access to our attendees? 

  1. Will all of the packages we ship down in the week before the event show up on time? 

  1. Will our speakers come through for us, prepared and energetic? 

  1. Will the hotel and travel experience be good? 

And we are doing all of that while running a law practice that gets busier and busier every day. 

 

Know what we didn’t plan for? 

 

The experience of the very last exercise we did on Saturday morning.  

 

A sort of quiet, personal, and then mastermind workshop, doing some deep thinking of what the next year would bring. The stories told from the stage during the three days of the Summit, I think, opened a lot of lawyers’ eyes as to the possibilities, not just with their practices, but with their lives. 

 

So it was pretty neat to see the “unplanned.” 

 

After Brian and I walked off the stage, no one left quickly. Some stayed for over an hour, talking with new friends, exchanging idea on how how they each could remove resistance from their lives. 

 

Resistance comes in many forms. Some will go back to the office today and realize they don’t have the right people on the bus. Maybe their teams can be coached up, maybe not. Decisions will have to be made if they are to live a life by design, not default. 

 

Many will realize that the resistance is inside their own minds. They came to Orlando, saw and heard from others who had burst through a limited belief mindset to reach new levels. These lawyers will begin to deliberately search out the books, groups, and people that can move them forward.
